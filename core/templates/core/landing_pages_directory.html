The Unconcerned Complete

Educated Professionals Who Find Workarounds
"Tired of complaining into the void? Make your voice count beyond your neighbor's gate."
Target Audience
Affluent, educated FCT residents (25-45) who typically solve problems individually through alternative services. They avoid complaining and prefer personal solutions.
Key Psychology
Sophistication, efficiency, collective impact. Appeals to their desire for systemic solutions rather than personal workarounds.
View Design
2

The Frustrated Activists Complete

People Who Complain But Feel Unheard
"Tired of shouting into silence? Finally, a platform that listens and acts."
Target Audience
Active complainers who engage on social media, write to authorities, or participate in community discussions but feel frustrated by lack of response.
Key Psychology
Urgency, empowerment, validation. Red color scheme conveys passion and action. Focuses on amplifying their existing voice.
View Design
3

The Concerned Parents Complete

Family-Focused Safety & Community Builders
"Your children deserve better. Build the community they'll be proud to call home."
Target Audience
Parents with children in FCT who prioritize family safety, clean environment, and community welfare. Motivated by legacy and children's future.
Key Psychology
Protection, stability, future-building. Blue conveys trust and safety. Emphasizes family values and community building.
View Design
4

The Hopeful Change-Makers Complete

Young Professionals & Students
"Your generation can fix this. Be the change FCT desperately needs."
Target Audience
University students, recent graduates, young professionals (22-32) who believe in innovation and progressive change. Tech-savvy and optimistic.
Key Psychology
Innovation, leadership, generational responsibility. Purple conveys creativity and progress. Appeals to their desire to lead change.
